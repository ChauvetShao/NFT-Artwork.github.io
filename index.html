Everything You Want to Know About NFT Art Is Right Here

NFT art is reshaping the value and market of art and attracting more people into this emerging field.

A banana taped to a wall with silver duct tape was auctioned, drawing a crowd of onlookers eager to snap photos of this seemingly absurd scene. This unusual event took place on November 24, 2024, and the banana was actually a conceptual artwork. Ultimately, this "one-of-a-kind" banana was sold for an astonishing $6.24 million to Justin Sun, the founder of Tron, an NFT trading platform.

After tasting this "priceless" banana, Sun shared two unique insights on social media: first, the banana was incredibly delicious; second, the banana no longer existed and had entered a decentralized state—a subtle connection between art and NFT.

You must be curious—what exactly NFTs are, and how do they relate to art?

NFT

In simple terms, anything that can be transformed into a "digital" format can become an NFT. A doodle, a photo, a video clip, a GIF, music, gaming items, selfies, or even a status update on social media—all these can be turned into NFTs and traded online using cryptocurrency.

What sets NFTs apart from other digital forms is their foundation in blockchain technology.

Blockchain is like a secure, digital ledger that keeps track of transactions in a decentralized and transparent way. Imagine it as a public library where every book is carefully recorded, and once it’s logged, nothing can be deleted or changed. This unchangeable quality guarantees that every NFT—whether it’s a painting, a song, or a video—is one of a kind, tied directly to the artist’s limitless creativity.

NFT art transforms how we perceive and interact with creativity. Let’s dive into these two game-changing aspects.

Redefining Art's Value

Imagine a world where art isn’t confined to canvas but thrives on the blockchain. Blockchain acts as a permanent digital diary, recording every detail of an artwork’s journey. Take Beeple’s Everydays: The First 5000 Days (will be detailed in the NFT artwork section) as an example—this masterpiece, created over 5000 consecutive days, with its entire ownership and exhibition history transparently logged on the blockchain.

In this digital art village, every piece comes with its own unique storybook. It might detail who created it, who owned it, and even where it’s been displayed online or in real life. This transparency brings decentralization to life, making each work of art more than just an image—it’s a slice of history. It is the story behind the artwork that enhances its value.

Reshaping the Art Market

Justin Sun, the proud owner of the million-dollar banana, believes NFTs liberate art from the confines of galleries and private collections, making it accessible to a wider audience and unlocking unprecedented possibilities.

NFTs have opened up new opportunities for digital artists. Take Fewocious as an example. Early on, his bold and vibrant art faced harsh rejection—even from family members, who thought his work was "ugly." However, when he uploaded his creations to the NFT community, his unique style quickly gained recognition. He sold his first piece for $3,000 and went on to establish a studio in Seattle, becoming a successful digital artist. In 2021, his work Hello, I’m Victor (FEWOCiOUS) sold for $2.2 million at Christie’s in New York.

Fewocious, Hello,I’m Victor (FEWOCiOUS).Source:christies

The NFT Market：From Hype to Maturity

The NFT boom of 2020 and 2021 saw investors rushing in during pandemic lockdowns, drawn by the promise of quick profits and digital ownership. But as the market became oversaturated, interest faded, and trading volumes plummeted. Was it just a fad?

By 2024, the answer is clear: NFTs are evolving. The frenzy has given way to a focus on quality, with high-value projects thriving and low-effort, speculative tokens fading out. No longer just a trend, NFTs are solidifying their place as a cornerstone of the digital era. The hype may be over, but the story is far from finished.

Can NFTs Really Make Money?

On OpenSea, the world’s largest NFT marketplace, sales data from a single week show that about one-third of NFTs sold for less than $100, and fewer than one-fifth sold for more than $300. While some top sellers earn large sums, most creators and collectors see only modest profits. This situation makes us wonder: can the NFT market become more balanced and offer fair opportunities for a wider range of artists and buyers in the future?

Who Makes Sure Transactions Are Safe?

Right now, the laws and regulations for NFTs are still playing catch-up with the fast pace of new technologies, leaving buyers and sellers with limited legal protection. In April, for example, scammers used what seemed like “free-mint” NFTs to trick people into paying hidden fees and even steal personal information. The criminals behind these schemes have often slipped through the cracks of existing laws. To make the NFT art market safer and more trustworthy, governments around the world urgently need to set up clear rules and guidelines.

What happens next —— opportunities and challenges

As technology continues to evolve, the way we create and experience NFT art is set to become even more immersive and diverse. The introduction of AI-generated content (AIGC)——tools that use artificial intelligence to create digital works—is transforming the NFT landscape. With these tools, anyone can design their own NFT art and share it on trading platforms. This means the creation and circulation of NFT artworks will be more dynamic than ever.

Anna Riddler, Mosaic Virus:An AI-generated NFT artwork combines intricate artistry with data visualization. Source:annaridler

Like any technological breakthrough, NFTs come with their share of challenges—and one of the biggest is their environmental impact. Did you know that creating a single NFT can consume as much electricity as powering a home for three months? That’s a staggering thought! This has sparked calling for more eco-friendly solutions. Thankfully, we’re already seeing some promising efforts, like blockchain networks designed to use far less energy and projects that actively work to offset their carbon footprint.

So, what’s next? Imagine a future where NFTs not only dazzle us with their creativity but also respect our planet. With advancements in green technology, the NFT world could soon deliver incredible artistic experiences without the heavy environmental cost. If the balance between innovation and sustainability can be struck, NFTs might just lead the way to a brighter, greener digital art revolution.

Every Day: the First 5,000 Days.

The author of this piece, Beeple, has brought some of the ideas and colours together into one big picture.As you zoom in, the images become increasingly abstract, fantastical or grotesque, either showing something very personal or representing what's going on in the world today. The work was eventually sold for $69.3 million in an online auction, making Beeple one of the world's most expensive artists.

Beeple, Everydays: the First 5000 Days.Source: barrons

Mario Klingemann, Memories of Passersby I. Source: bloomberg

Memories of Passersby I

A landmark in the history of art. It's fully autonomous and uses a complex system of neural networks to generate a never-ending stream of portraits. These are disquieting visions of male and female faces created by a machine. The artwork is presented as an installation piece. The creator, Klingemann, says the art is not the images themselves, but rather the computer code that creates them.

Archillet

Archillet is an innovative form of digital artwork created by anonymous artist Pak. It is an AI built to discover and share stimulating visual media. It searches the web for popular content using keywords, posts an image and adjusts the next one based on user reactions. The images are mostly in shades of grey, blue and black, with some geometric lines that seem to embody the cold essence of the machine.

Works posted by Archillet.Source: Archillet

Anadol, Artificial Realities: Coral.Photo courtesy of Refik Anadol Studios.Source:artnet

Artificial Realities: Coral

This is a multi-channel auditory and visual experience artwork created using AI image generation technology. The artist, Anadol, trained a unique AI model on nearly 500 million coral images , crafting new visual effects and color combinations. Immersed in this environment, viewers feel as if they've entered a vibrant coral world. Through this piece, Anadol aims to raise awareness about protecting the marine ecosystem.

Bored Ape Yacht Club

It's an NFT collection featuring 10,000 unique ape avatars, each with their own distinct traits.The creators of it chose the imagery of "bored apes" to convey a sense of humor, rebellion, and cultural resonance with the NFT community. If you own a Bored Ape, you'll get exclusive perks like access to private online spaces, special events, and future benefits.It has become a cultural phenomenon, attracting celebrities and NFT enthusiasts alike.

10 of the 10,000 Bored Ape Yacht Club NFTs. Each has different attributes, some rarer than others, that makes them unique. Source: cnet

There are many online platforms for buying, selling and creating NFTs.
Each marketplace offers unique features such as royalty sharing, dynamic art creation and multi-chain support to meet the diverse needs of artists, collectors and investors.

OpenSea

The world's first and largest NFT marketplace. Ideal for NFT collectors and beginners as it is easy to use and resourceful.

SuperRare

A high-end NFT marketplace focused on rare, single-edition digital art. Suitable for professional artists and high-end collectors.

Rarible

A platform that allows users to create and sell NFTs on their own, for those who love community exchange.

As NFT art grows and develops, we're seeing a number of new platforms combine NFT with technologies like AR to create flexible and innovative social media applications. It uses an AR app and website to give street artists a safe place to create and display their work without damaging public property. By connecting with NFTs, it turns physical street art into digital assets.
